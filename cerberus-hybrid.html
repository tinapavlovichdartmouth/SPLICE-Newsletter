Announcements

  • Students, PostDocs, Junior Faculty: After our all-hands meeting this past November, SPLICE profs discussed availability of funding for conference travel. Each of the SPLICE PIs are committed to sending their "SPLICE graduate students, postdoctoral scholars, and junior faculty [...] to [...] at least one conference to network and connect with [...] professionals experienced in their field" (SPLICE proposal). If you have a publication accepted at a conference or find a conference relevant to your work, please ask your faculty advisor about attending the conference. Along with funding being available through SPLICE, students, postdocs, and junior faculty are encouraged to look and apply for travel grants provided by the conference itself, their academic departments, as well as professional organizations. We encourage you to discuss whether these additional funding opportunities exist with your advisors, peers, and graduate program administrators. Thank you to the students and postdocs who brought this topic to our attention. If you have any additional questions, please feel free to reach out to me (Tina) or your advisor.
  • SPLICE Outreach: Publishing in and presenting at conferences is one form of outreach. As SPLICE research develps and matures, part of our goal is to increase our outreach -- informing the community and the world about our findings -- including reaching the general public. One outlet for such work is The Conversation. The Conversation is an online site that publishes opinion pieces by academics. It is a non-profit, funded by foundations, universities, and the general public. Its purpose is to spread information about academic research more widely. The Conversation uses a Creative Commons license allowing reuse so long as the piece is published in full. SPLICE PI Susan Landau (Tufts) has published several pieces in The Conversation. Some have reappeared in local papers, while others have been reprinted in online versions of major ones, including the Los Angeles Times. Pieces would have to be of relatively wide interest; a very technical fix or attack would not do well. The Conversation provides professional editors to polish submissions. Faculty and PhD students can publish in The Conversation, although the site does not currenctly accept pieces by master's students. Information about writing for The Conversation is here.
  • SPLICE project group meetings: Are you part of a cross-institutional SPLICE project? If yes, please let me know, even if you are scheduling your own meetings. Knowing that these meetings are happening will help me in preparing the SPLICE annual report. And if your project group would like assistance in scheduling recurring meetings, please reach out to me!
